Frustrated Superradiant Phase Transition

Jinchen Zhao, Myung-Joong Hwang

Frustration is an interesting phenomenon in spin physics, where the system cannot minimize its energy due to conflicting constraints. We show that the order parameters during a superradiant phase transition can also become frustrated. This can lead to many exotic critical behaviors, such as the emergence of two diverging time scales.

[Physical Review Letters] [arXiv]

Anomalous criticality with bounded fluctuations and long-range frustration induced by broken time-reversal symmetry

Jinchen Zhao, Myung-Joong Hwang

We identify an anomalous superradiant phase transition in a synthetic magnetic field. Due to the breaking of time-reversal symmetry, the system can exhibit a series of first-order transitions as we tune the magnetic flux. Moreover, the system displays novel behaviors in the normal phase, such as a bounded photon number, in contrast to the divergent photon number observed in other superradiant phase transitions.
